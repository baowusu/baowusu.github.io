About

I joined Dublin Institute for Advanced Studies in 2024 as a PhD Student in Seismology and Temperature Modelling under the supervision of Dr Emma Chambers. My PhD research is part of the MOD3LTHERM Project and is funded under the Research Ireland Pathway Programme. I am also registered as a PhD Student at the School of Earth Sciences, University College Dublin.

As a PhD Student on the MOD3LTHERM Project, the goal of my research is to develop new workflows to accurately assess the geothermal potential in both low and high enthalpy geothermal regions in the world, with primary focus on Krafla, Iceland. I am developing a new geophysical-petrological-lithological inversion scheme and applyling the method to a local-scale application at Krafla, a high enthalpy geothermal region in Iceland.

Bio

I started a PhD in the Autumn of 2024 at the Dublin Institute for Advanced Studies and University College Dublin. I studied Physics (B.Sc. '20) and Geophysics (M.Phil. '23) at the Kwame Nkrumah University of Science and Technology. During the summer of 2023, I had the opportunity of working at the Geophysics Department, Ghana National Petroleum Corporation. From 2023 to 2024, I worked as a Graduate Research Assistant at the Department of Physics, Kwame Nkrumah University of Science and Technology.
