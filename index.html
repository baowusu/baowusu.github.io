Bio

I am currently involved in the MOD3LTHERM Project which aims to develop new workflows to accurately assess the geothermal potential in both low and high enthalpy geothermal regions in the world. My research focuses on developing a new geophysical-petrological-lithological inversion scheme and applyling same for a local-scale application at a high enthalpy geothermal region - Krafla, Iceland.

I am a PhD Student at the Geophysics Section, Dublin Institute for Advanced Studies, working under the supervision of Dr Emma Chambers. My PhD is funded under the MOD3LTHERM Project, SFI-IRC (Research Ireland) Pathways Programme. I am also registered as a PhD Student at the University College Dublin.

I previously studied Geophysics at the Kwame Nkrumah University of Science and Technology. I focused on exploration seismology and machine learning applications in the Ghanaian offshore region with Dr. Cyril D. Boateng. During the summer of 2023, I worked with the Geophysics Department of the Ghana National Petroleum Corporation.

I enjoy listening to good music. I am a huge fun of choral, church, and classical music and my favorite groups are the Choir of the King's College, Cambridge and Harmonious Chorale, Ghana. I also enjoy playing games, art, and reading on history.
