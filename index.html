About

I am a PhD Student at the Geophysics Sectionb>, Dublin Institute for Advanced Studies, working on research in the MOD3LTHERM Group. My PhD is under the supervision of Dr Emma Chambers and funded under the MOD3LTHERM Project by the Research Ireland Pathway Programme. I am also registered as a PhD Student at the School of Earth Sciences, University College Dublin.

As a PhD Student on the MOD3LTHERM Project, the goal of my research is to develop new workflows to accurately assess the geothermal potential in both low and high enthalpy geothermal regions in the world. I am developing a new geophysical-petrological-lithological inversion scheme and applyling the method to a local-scale application at Krafla, a high enthalpy geothermal region in Iceland to determine the subsurface geothermal structure. This will help improve the way we use indirect measurements in determining subsurface geothermal potential.

Bio

I started a PhD in the Autumn of 2024 at the Dublin Institute for Advanced Studies and University College Dublin. Previously, I obtained Bachelor and Masters degrees in Physics and Geophysics respectively from the Kwame Nkrumah University of Science and Technology. During the summer of 2023, I had the opportunity of working at the Geophysics Department, Ghana National Petroleum Corporation. From 2023 to 2024, I was also appointed Graduate Research Assistant at the Department of Physics, Kwame Nkrumah University of Science and Technology.
